# 2026 Board Meeting Minutes of the OSS Compass Community
## Time
14:00–18:00, Afternoon, June 2, 2026 (Beijing Time)

## Meeting Minutes
### I. Election of New Board Members
The election for new Board members was held at the meeting. Five authoritative experts from leading universities, industrial standard-setting institutions and international open source organizations were elected. All attending Board members reviewed and voted unanimously to approve the proposal, whereby the five experts officially joined the OSS-Compass Governing Board to inject specialized expertise into the community’s ecosystem development.

Yang Liyun, Director of the Open Source Research Division, Software Center of China Electronics Standardization Institute, delivered a speech. With years of experience in standardization for cloud computing and open source, she has facilitated the development of open source standard frameworks, presided over the formulation and release of multiple national, industrial and group standards in the open source field, and participated in core initiatives of numerous international open source foundations and committees to advance the implementation of open source governance standards. Moving forward, she will prioritize the international standardization of the OSS-Compass indicator system. Leveraging her academic findings and cross-industry cooperation resources, she will deepen partnerships with global open source organizations to advance the internationalization of the OSS-Compass framework.

Guo Xue, Director of the Open Source & Software Security Department, Cloud Computing and Digital Economy Research Institute, China Academy of Information and Communications Technology, shared her insights. She has long been engaged in open source industry research, taking a leading role in open source policy research, industrial standard formulation and ecological development, and spearheaded standardization for multiple flagship open source projects. Following her board appointment, she will participate deeply in the construction and optimization of OSS-Compass’s project evaluation framework and core models. Relying on the platform’s bridging function, she will drive the industrial and governmental adoption of OSS-Compass outcomes to support open source selection for central and state-owned enterprises as well as local open source ecosystem construction. In addition, she will mobilize resources from standardization bodies, industrial communities and agent-focused open source communities to build a regular cooperation mechanism with OSS-Compass for improved open source governance and core technology iteration.

Associate Professor Ni Chao from the School of Software Technology, Zhejiang University, stated that his research team focuses on core technologies for intelligent software quality and efficiency assurance alongside open source talent cultivation and industry-university-research innovation in higher education. The team independently developed the Taiyi Platform (https://link.wtturl.cn/?target=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.taiyi.top&scene=im&aid=497858&lang=zh ), a three-party collaborative hub linking universities, industrial enterprises and open source communities to boost open source education. To resolve the mismatch between university training curricula and corporate talent demands, the platform connects students with enterprises and open source communities via discipline competitions, customized courses and practical events, strengthening students’ hands-on capabilities and boosting their engagement in open source projects. Ni Chao noted that his team will cooperate with OSS-Compass to scale up open source competitions, cultivate high-caliber open source talents aligned with real industrial demands and unlock the innovation potential of university open source initiatives.

Daniel Izquierdo, Researcher at OpenHQ and Board Member of the CHAOSS Project under the Linux Foundation, has devoted his career to the global open source landscape with extensive participation in top-tier international open source bodies and research on open source development patterns and global ecosystem application. He highlighted the pivotal value of cross-border open source collaboration and emphasized open source technologies as critical enablers for digital transformation across government, enterprise and social sectors. He expressed willingness to build a robust long-term partnership with OSS-Compass through cross-regional data sharing and joint evaluation model development to foster standardized and sustainable global open source advancement.

Zheng Zibin, Dean of the School of Software, Sun Yat-sen University, specializes in compliant evaluation of large language models. Upon joining the community, he plans to build an industry-university-research collaborative standard system for trustworthy LLM evaluation based on the neutral positioning of open source foundations and vast developer ecosystems. He will facilitate the rollout of LLM evaluation indicators, core algorithms and dedicated evaluation platforms via shared standard infrastructure, dedicated R&D staffing and ecological exchange events.
### II. Progress Report: Evaluation Model Working Group
Wang Yehui, OSS-Compass Board Member and Co-Chair of the Technical Committee, presented updates from the Evaluation Model Working Group. Against the backdrop of widespread AI adoption and persistent identity management challenges within open source communities, he analyzed emerging shifts and challenges facing open source community development and software engineering. AI-driven development is reshaping open source industry rules, which necessitates upgrades to existing open source governance rules, infrastructure, evaluation systems and engineering frameworks. Going forward, the Working Group will target pain points in AI-era open source governance, upgrade governance rules and infrastructure tailored for intelligent scenarios, iterate open source evaluation and engineering systems, and explore innovative industry-university-research partnerships plus intelligent evaluation tool development to accommodate the new AI-powered open source development ecosystem.
### III. Progress Report: AI Working Group
Qi Guoqiang, Lead of OSS-Compass AI Working Group, reported on the development and deployment of the Developer Experience Evaluation Platform, detailing its technical architecture, core evaluation models, task orchestration logic and future roadmap. Centered on the full lifecycle of developer community participation, the platform defines six core user journeys paired with refined assessment tasks to comprehensively evaluate developers’ project experience and coding efficiency. Adopting a lightweight modular design, the platform splits complex evaluation tasks into discrete modules, generates customized assessment plans via intelligent orchestration, and automatically outputs professional evaluation reports upon task completion. The platform has pinpointed key bottlenecks hampering open source contributors, supports dynamic issue tracking and real-time updates, and enables interactive developer feedback to enhance practical usability. The Working Group will continuously integrate cutting-edge AI technologies to further refine the platform’s intelligent and granular evaluation capabilities.
### IV. Establishment of Open-Source LLM Compliance Working Group
The Open-Source Large Model Compliance Working Group was formally founded at the meeting. Yan Xuanchen, Senior Engineer at Huawei, led the presentation of the working group’s core objectives and roadmap, examining prevailing industry bottlenecks across four dimensions: copyright, privacy, cybersecurity and value compliance. Current domestic open-source LLM evaluation is hindered by opaque test set origins, poor regional adaptability, insufficient compliance-oriented model fine-tuning and oversimplified scoring criteria, restricting orderly industrial growth. To address these gaps, the working group aims to develop an open, neutral, reusable and implementable set of compliance standards for open-source LLMs, and promote engineering implementation of compliance algorithms together with universities, tech firms and open source communities. Planned milestones include phased development, optimization and rollout of a dedicated compliance evaluation platform alongside participation in international standard-setting, ultimately forming a widely recognized cross-industry compliance assessment framework to improve the copyright, privacy and cybersecurity compliance of open-source large models.
